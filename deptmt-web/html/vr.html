What is Virtual Reality?

  It is the computer-generated simulation of a three-dimensional image or environment that can be interacted with in a seemingly real or physical way by a person using special electronic equipment, such as a helmet with a screen inside or gloves fitted with sensors.

Effective uses of Virtual Reality:

  • Recruitment and Training
  • Training Medical Students
  • Therapy for Paraplegics
  • VR in Military
  • VR in fashion
  • VR in education
  • Cloud, Cost-Effective in Nature
  • Cloud Architecture is Exceptionally Flexible

Job roles:

  Virtual reality market is soaring and is expected to reach $215 billion by 2021, as per the recent study. Demand for skilled virtual reality professionals are on the rise, as more and more technologies make it out of the research and development departments and enter the market place. Positions for which virtual reality professionals are being hired are:

1.Content producer

  Like other visual storytellers, the role of a VR-content producer is to create an experience that has an emotional resonance with audiences, only with the additional tools, challenges and opportunities that come with a new medium.

2.Software engineer

  Virtual reality engineering includes the use of 3D modelling tools and visualisation techniques as part of the design process. This technology enables engineers to view their project in 3D and gain a greater understanding of how it works. Plus they can spot any flaws or potential risks before implementation.

3.Product managers

  Product manager is a professional role that is responsible for the development of products for an organization, known as the practice of product management. Product managers own the business strategy behind a product , specify its functional requirements, and generally manage the launch of features. They coordinate work done by many other functions (like software engineers, data scientists, and product designers) and are ultimately responsible for the business success of the product

4.UI and UX designer

  User experience (UX) design is the process design teams use to create products that provide meaningful and relevant experiences to users. This involves the design of the entire process of acquiring and integrating the product, including aspects of branding, design, usability and function.
